My First Blog Post

By Daniel Ndanema

January 24, 2022

497 words – 3 min read

I have spent the past few days pouring all my learned skills in building this blog page on my mock site. I was a bit skeptical about the approach I would use because I was pretty much stuck and undecided. I had so many options to choose from and I had become boggled down by the sheer enormity of the resources available to me. I have spent quite a considerable amount of time (over a year now) learning about website creation; though I wasn't as consistent as I would've loved to be.

In my readings, it has always occurred to me that I needed to start doing and stop consuming tons of information. Often times, I would engage in a few self-driven projects, but I could not muster the consistency to enable me learn deeply and truly. At certain times, it was simply bits and pieces of exercises I had laboured over, that I would recollect for my efforts. There was nothing tangible I could point to that I had done of which I could be proud. I would berate myself over and again because I was cognizant of my lack of effort in aspiring to produce/create some quality work that might reflect my "growing" knowledge.

The past couple months have made me realize the importance of taking the initiative and being responsible for my life. It has tortured me into developing a mindset of self-sufficiency and personal growth. Getting up and reaching out for my own good is a critical component in developing resilience and self-reliance in my life. No one's coming to save me!Being alone has provided me an opportunity to have a glance in the mirror; to see what is there; and to summon the will to admit what should be.

Building this blog post has made me – once again – realize how important it is to create. How much of me it has taken – my energy, my will, and my desire to produce something better than I always have. From setting up the layout; choosing the right fonts; deciding on which colours to use; what kind of element would fit in certain contexts; and so much more, it has taken me through my existing domain of expertise and acquainted me with novel ideas and concepts. I now understand what it means to do. Doing opens the eyes as it does the mind. It opens up new opportunities to learn and grow. It creates an avenue for pragmatism and practicality. It shows my weakness(es); it enables me practice intellectual humility.Practice makes perfect progress!

Hence, so it's been for the past two-and-a-half days, putting the final touches on what might end up being the final layout for my long awaited blog post. It's been a long time coming and I have procrastinated for too long. It was a matter of time; I have made it possible by bringing the time nearer. Taking the initiative, and doing what is right. For my own good!
